Intel’s AI Advantage

As a standout feature of the latest Intel® Core™ Ultra processors, AI (Artificial Intelligence) works within PCs to perform typical tasks in a way that mimics human intelligence.

By taking in large amounts of data, AI learns patterns and how to process information in a way that simulates users. Based on the data, AI can create an interface to anticipate users’ decisions and improve their task efficiency.

Advanced AI capabilities enable Intel® Core™ Ultra processors to provide the increased processing speeds and power efficiency that propel PCs into the future.

Intel’s AI Engines

Intel® Core™ Ultra processors utilize 3 different architecture tiles to deliver AI power. The CPU, GPU, and NPU (Neural Processing Unit) can be used individually or together to accomplish AI workloads.

CPU

AI-optimized CPUs are ideal for smaller, low-latency workloads.

Example: Noise suppression

During a Microsoft Teams video call, the CPU will automatically use AI to suppress background noise.

GPU

Large content creation workloads can be expedited by the GPUs AI.

Example: Image generation

High-throughput workloads such as these include generative imagery. After receiving a large amount of information to process, the GPU can create a realistic image.

NPU (Neural Processing Unit)

Built into the newest generation of Intel® Core™ Ultra processors, NPUs enable efficiency by handling longer-running AI workloads at low power.

Example: Speech-to-text

While listening to a coworker present on a Zoom call, the NPU could perform sustained AI-powered speech to text and transcribe what your coworker is presenting.
